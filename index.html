Research & Foundations

Our methodologies in algorithmic trading and quantitative finance are deeply rooted in established academic research and emerging machine learning paradigms. Below are key references that inform our approach:

  • The integration of machine learning with quantitative finance has emerged as a powerful paradigm for real-time market prediction and portfolio optimization.
  • Machine learning models can incorporate diverse data sources such as historical price movements, trading volumes, and macroeconomic indicators.
  • In their 2013 publication "Machine Learning for Market Microstructure and High Frequency Trading," Kearns and Nevmyvaka survey the practical application of machine learning to trading problems utilizing microstructure data.
  • Fischer and Krauss (2018) authored the highly referenced paper "Deep learning with long short-term memory networks for financial market predictions," demonstrating the efficacy of sequence models in quantitative finance.
